Coffee types

Cappuccino

Italian coffee, cappuccino is an espresso-based java that comes from an Austrian drink called kapuziner

Cortado

A smaller version of a cappuccino, Roasty Coffee says that a cortado has just enough milk "to cut" the espresso and its acidity.

Latte

the latte was fashioned to be a less intense and considerably more milky version of a cappuccino for not-so-strong coffee-loving Americans.

Mocha

mocha is made from similar layers of espresso, milk, and chocolate.

Raf

Raf coffee originated in mid-1990 Russia when Rafael Timerbaev walked into a Coffee Bean outlet asking for a "good cup of coffee with milk"

Macchiato

a macchiato is a shot of espresso with just a dash of milk to mellow its intensity

Cold Brew

cold brew is made by steeping coffee grounds in cold or room temperature water for at least 12 hours

Espresso Con Panna

Espresso con panna is a shot of espresso served with a swirl of whipped cream on top.

Turkish coffee

Turkish coffee, or Türk kahvesi, is brewed in a unique metal pot with a long handle called cezve (via Lonely Planet). Instead of a flame or an electric machine, the coffee is let to boil in the cezve over hot sand till it just about spills, and is then served in a standard-sized cup.
